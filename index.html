About the Show

The global phenomenon that redefined survival drama

A Modern Masterpiece

Squid Game is a South Korean survival drama television series created by Hwang Dong-hyuk for Netflix. The series revolves around a contest where 456 players, all of whom are in deep financial hardship, risk their lives to play a series of deadly children's games for the chance to win a ₩45.6 billion prize.

Released in 2021, the show quickly became Netflix's most-watched series, attracting more than 142 million member households during its first four weeks, surpassing Bridgerton as the network's most-watched show.

The series has been praised for its originality, creativity, acting, visual style, musical score, and commentary on social inequality. It has attracted international attention and won numerous accolades, including the Golden Globe Award for Best Supporting Actor for O Yeong-su.

The Deadly Games

Childhood games turned into life-or-death challenges

Red Light, Green Light

The first game in the series, this traditional children's game takes a deadly turn. When the giant robot doll calls "Green Light," players can move toward the finish line. But when she calls "Red Light" and turns around, anyone still moving is immediately eliminated – not just from the game, but from life itself.

The doll uses motion sensors to detect movement, and players have just five minutes to cross the finish line. The psychological horror of this game sets the tone for the entire series, as contestants realize the true stakes of the competition.

Honeycomb / Dalgona Candy

In this game, players must cut out a shape from a fragile honeycomb candy using only a needle. The shapes vary in difficulty: umbrella, star, circle, and triangle. If the shape breaks during extraction, the player is eliminated.

This game tests precision and patience under extreme pressure. Gi-hun discovers that licking the back of the candy helps melt it slightly, making the task easier – a clever strategy that saves his life.

Tug of War

Teams of ten face off in a traditional tug-of-war match on elevated platforms. The losing team plummets to their death. This game highlights the importance of strategy over pure strength.

Il-nam shares his knowledge: positioning team members alternating left and right for better grip, starting in a leaning position to hold ground, and waiting for the opponent to tire before pulling. Despite being physically weaker, Gi-hun's team overcomes their opponents using these tactics.
